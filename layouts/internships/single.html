About the Program

Two Streams, Endless Possibilities

The Kartoza Internship prepares candidates for the workplace and builds skills, knowledge, experience, and portfolios through real-world projects.

We offer two streams: GIS Intern and Developer Intern. There's significant overlap between them - GIS interns gain coding exposure while developers learn GIS fundamentals.

Kartoza interns walk away with new or extended skills, real-world experience, and exposure to the operations of a small but dynamic and successful business.

Requirements

What You'll Need

Minimum qualifications to join our program

Education

Minimum three-year undergraduate degree or diploma in Geography, GIS, Geoinformatics, Computer Science, Mathematics, Physics, Remote Sensing, Environmental Sciences, IT, or related fields.

Equipment

Your own computer and access to high-speed broadband Internet for remote collaboration.

Commitment

40 hours a week on a flexitime basis. We understand you might be studying, but need close to full-time commitment.

Attitude

A willingness to learn, communicate effectively, and impress. Curiosity and initiative are highly valued.

Exceptions

We may consider school students, school-leavers, or candidates without an undergraduate degree at our discretion.

Duration

Minimum one month, typically three months. Extended month-by-month if both parties agree. Small monthly stipend provided.

Curriculum

What You'll Learn

A structured program with real-world experience

Week 1: Foundation

Onboarding, induction, getting to know the staff, learning remote communication, and self-study tasks.

  • QGIS Beginners course, simple maps, and QGIS artworks
  • PostGIS Database concepts and basic SQL manipulation
  • GeoNode Data storage, access, and online map creation
  • Cartography Beautiful maps with tips and tricks from the team

Weeks 2-4 and Beyond

Based on your skills and interests, you'll be assigned to real project teams working on actual client work.

Real-world projects interns have contributed to:

  • Clean Cooking Explorer Global energy access mapping
  • Risk Informed Response Platform Disaster response coordination
  • GeoNode Documentation Open source contribution
  • Kartoza Training Material Course content development

Job Shadowing

Vac Work & School Programs

Exploring what a career in GIS looks like? We can help.

We frequently host Grade 11 or 12 students for job shadowing days, helping them understand what 'GIS' or a 'real-world IT business' is all about.

Job shadowing is allowed at our discretion and when feasible, as all of our team works remotely. For undergrad students seeking direction on specialisation, major subjects, or postgrad choices, the internship is the recommended path.

School-leavers and students seeking vac work: Please apply for an internship if you qualify!
